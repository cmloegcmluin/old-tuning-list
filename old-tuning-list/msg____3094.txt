Ben:  Welcome to the List!

Schlesinger's theory is unlikely to have been true for Greek music 
at any date, but her scales are fascinating in their own right.  
Her theory suffers from questionable historical evidence, internal 
inconsistencies, and an insufficient appreciation of playing techniques 
and the vagaries of wind instruments. Her arguments re notation seem 
circular to me. The material she presents on non-western music is 
interesting and may be correct, at least in part, but is of questionable 
relevance.  However, as I stated above, her scales are of considerable 
interest.

I have found the two books listed below to be the best available:

 Ancient Greek Music.  M. L. West. Clarendon Press, Oxford,
1992, xiii, 410 pp., illus.  ISBN 0-19-814897-6.
 
 Greek Musical Writings.  Volume II,  Harmonic and Acoustic Theory. 
Andrew Barker. Cambridge University Press, 1989, viii, 581 pp., 
ISBN 0-521-30220-X

Though old, this slim volume is a good description of Greek modality. 

Winnington-Ingram, R.P. 1936.   Mode in Ancient Greek Music, reprinted 
1968.  Adolf M. Hakkert. Amsterdam.
