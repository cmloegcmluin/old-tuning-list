>I completely agree with your wife. First of all, Mozart can't be played in
>just intonation. Secondly, there is no correspondence between our conscious
>manipulation of numerical quantities and the psychoacoustic phenomena related
>to small integers. Thirdly, appreciation of Mozart requires not only passive
>listenening but also memory and the ability to keep some thoughts in one's
>mind while others are being worked out, and then to bring all these thoughts
>together. I would suspect not only mathematical but generally any logical,
>deductive thinking would be enhanced by early exposure to Mozart.
