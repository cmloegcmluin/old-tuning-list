Gary Morrison wrote,

>}Based upon what I do know of the topic, here's the answer:  Unlike in
>}light, there's no simple correlation between higher vibratory frequencies
>}in an audio system and higher energy.  Light is an absolutely direct form
>}of energy, whereas sound is a much more complicated and round-about
>}physical manifestation of energy.  It involves pressure in a medium, which
>}is based upon collisions of randomly-moving molecules in the medium, and so
>}forth.  So you can't apply that equation to audio;  the wavelength (nu)
>}term in "E = H nu" doesn't apply to sound waves.
>
>Gary, this is not correct. E = H nu applies to all energy quanta, whether in
>the form of light, sound, or whatever. The quantum of light is the photon;
>the quantum of sound is the phonon. Just as sound can be viewed as
>oscillations in a medium, so can light -- the medium is the electromagnetic
>field. This wave-particle duality is quite unbelievable to anyone who has not
>studied quantum mechanics in depth; for those of us who have, the duality is
>easy to accept, but there are deeper mysteries that take its place (my
>favorite subject besides tuning).
